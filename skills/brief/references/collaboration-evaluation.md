# Collaboration Evaluation

Use this reference to evaluate how the candidate works with other engineers using public collaboration artifacts.

## Scope

- Review public PR threads, code reviews, issue discussions, and design discussions.
- Focus on behavior under disagreement, feedback quality, and delivery follow-through.
- Evaluate only public collaboration traces; do not infer private team behavior.

## Minimum evidence

- Target at least 2 collaboration artifacts when available.
- Include at least one example where the candidate gave feedback and one where they received feedback.
- If public collaboration artifacts are absent, mark `not-assessed`.

## What to inspect

- Review quality: specific, actionable, technically grounded comments.
- Responsiveness: timely follow-up, closure behavior, and ownership of unresolved items.
- Communication style: respectful tone, clarity, and ability to align contributors.
- Decision quality: whether tradeoffs are explained and decisions are documented.

## Authenticity checks

- Prefer multi-party threads with back-and-forth over isolated comments.
- Verify that interactions are recent and sustained, not a one-time spike.
- If collaboration surface is mostly absent, explicitly mark this component as limited.

## Not evaluable from public data

- Internal feedback style in private repos/channels.
- Cross-functional influence not reflected in public artifacts.

## Scoring guide (1-5)

- `5`: consistently strong technical collaboration with clear leadership and constructive behavior.
- `4`: strong collaboration with occasional gaps.
- `3`: mixed collaboration quality or limited sample depth.
- `2`: repeated weak communication or unresolved collaborative risks.
- `1`: high-risk collaboration signals with poor follow-through.

## Required output shape

- `Collaboration evaluation`: include `evidence sufficiency` (`assessed`, `limited`, or `not-assessed`), score when assessable, positives, risks, and a `References` subsection.
- `References`: at least 2 citations when available.
- Each citation should include URL + artifact pointer + short impact statement.
