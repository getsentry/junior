# Scoring Rubric

Use this rubric to produce a calibrated executive grade.

## Scoring Model

Score each dimension from 1 to 5, then compute a weighted total.

| Dimension | Weight | What "5" Looks Like |
|---|---:|---|
| Technical depth and ownership | 25% | Built meaningful systems and can defend tradeoffs with specifics |
| Career trajectory and execution | 20% | Consistent growth, clear impact, strong role-to-role progression |
| Communication and sharpness proxies | 20% | Clear, concise explanations; evidence of nuanced technical judgment |
| Public technical credibility | 15% | High-quality OSS, talks, papers, or other externally validated work |
| Collaboration and leadership proxies | 10% | Evidence of mentoring, reviews, cross-functional execution, conflict navigation |
| Tenure quality and durability | 10% | Stable history with intentional moves and minimal unexplained short stints |

## Grade Bands

Map weighted score to grade:

- 4.60-5.00: `A+`
- 4.30-4.59: `A`
- 4.00-4.29: `A-`
- 3.70-3.99: `B+`
- 3.30-3.69: `B`
- 3.00-3.29: `B-`
- 2.70-2.99: `C+`
- 2.30-2.69: `C`
- 2.00-2.29: `C-`
- 1.60-1.99: `D`
- <1.60: `F`

Use plus/minus only when evidence is sufficient for precision.

## Confidence Calibration

Assign overall confidence:

- `High`: broad primary-source coverage across all required signals
- `Medium`: moderate coverage, some inferred sections
- `Low`: sparse coverage or unresolved identity ambiguity

Reduce confidence when:

- Identity is uncertain
- Timeline has unresolved conflicts
- Public artifacts are too limited for a stable judgment

## Dave Rubric Mapping

Map executive signals into the scored dimensions:

- "Sharpness", staying on thread, concise answers:
  - Communication and sharpness proxies
- "Can they talk shop?", "did they build it?", risk analysis:
  - Technical depth and ownership
- Curiosity, informed opinions, disagreement quality:
  - Communication and sharpness proxies
  - Public technical credibility
- Collaboration, ambition, conflict handling:
  - Collaboration and leadership proxies
- Self-motivation, intellectual honesty, deliberate growth:
  - Career trajectory and execution
  - Tenure quality and durability

## Red and Green Flags

Strong green flags:

- Sustained ownership in complex systems
- Clear evidence of technical judgment under constraints
- Thoughtful public artifacts (talks, papers, OSS)
- Career moves that look deliberate and compounding

Red flags:

- Repeated short stints without clear context
- Inflated claims with weak artifact support
- Shallow technical artifacts despite senior titles
- Inconsistent timeline or identity ambiguity not resolved

## Final Narrative Rule

The grade must match the narrative:

- If risks are severe and repeated, grade cannot exceed `B-`.
- If evidence is outstanding but narrow, grade can be high with lowered confidence.
- Never hide uncertainty; uncertainty changes confidence first, grade second.
